Video: Intermediate Force Capabilities: Bringing the Fight to the Gray Zone

Lethality is absolutely necessary, but not sufficient to effectively engage across the competition continuum. Intermediate force capabilities (IFCs) enable combat arms (ex: Infantry, Armor, surface combatants) and combat service support warfighters to bridge the gap between presence and lethal effects/intent. IFCs must complement lethal force to yield enduring strategic victories for our warfighters and the Nation.

JNLWD Leadership Engages Armament Tech Industry at 2019 Armament Systems Forum (ASF)

One doesn’t typically associate NLW with small arms systems, rockets, and missiles, however JNLWD leadership accepted an invitation to discuss its mission at the National Defense Industrial Assoc.’s annual ASF this month. Colonel W.B. Leimbach, Jr., JNLWD Director; Kevin Swenson, Acquisitions Division Chief; and Dave Law, Chief Scientist, introduced attendees to the DoD NLW Program’s role in the 2018 U.S. National Defense Strategy, JNLWP Science and Technology Strategic Plan, as well as small arms and emerging armament investments. NLW provide operating forces with intermediate force capabilities between presence and lethal effects while minimizing casualties and collateral damage.

Advancements in SWaP-C to Deliver Smaller, Efficient Technology to the DOD’s Directed Energy (DE) Community

Advances in size, weight, power, and cooling (SWaP-C) components can deliver smaller, lighter, efficient, and less costly counter-personnel/materiel directed energy weapons into servicemembers’ hands at the speed of relevance. The JNLWD partners with the DOD RDT&E enterprise, industry, academia, and international agencies to develop and integrate radio frequency/high-power microwave, laser, e-steerable antennas, active denial, and other DE intermediate force weapons into military system designs.

Why Non-Lethal Weapons?

Non-lethal weapons (NLWs) deliver discriminate and reversible effects without causing unnecessary destruction or loss of life.  Skilled application of NLWs supports mission objectives by complementing lethal force without unintentionally initiating, escalating, or prolonging extensive (and expensive) hostilities.

Vessel Incapacitating Power Effect Radiation (VIPER) Program is a Research and Development Based Operational Utility Assessment

The Naval Surface Warfare Center, Dahlgren Division (NSWCDD) is issuing this sources sought and request for information synopsis as a means of conducting market research to identify parties having an interest to support the Vessel Incapacitating Power Effect Radiation (VIPER) program of the Joint Non-Lethal Weapons Directorate (JNLWD). The VIPER program is a research and development-based Operational Utility Assessment (OUA) sponsored by the JNLWD that will provide a non-lethal, potentially reversible vessel stopping capability for the United States Coast Guard (USCG) Law Enforcement community. This approach provides a safer and more effective means to subdue non-compliant vessels within a dynamic environment.

REPORTS ON THE PROTECTION OF CIVILIANS IN ARMED CONFLICT

United Nations Assistance Mission: Afghanistan (UNAMA) prepares regular reports in accordance with its UN Security Council mandate, undertaking a range of activities aimed at minimizing the impact of the armed conflict on civilians. Since 2012, the reports have been prepared jointly with the UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR). Reports using a consistent methodology have been maintained since 2009. Note that earlier reports from 2007 and 2008 follow a previous reporting system and are included here for reference purposes only.

PEVS system demonstrated for distinguished visitors

A car drives across the Pre-Emplaced Electric Vehicle Stopper to demonstrate its non-lethal stopping method on Feb. 13 in front of Tinker Air Force Base, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base and Ellsworth Air Force Base personnel, as well as members of the DOD Joint Non-Lethal Weapons Directorate and a representative from Oklahoma Senator James Inhofe’s office. Besides PEVS being a non-lethal, scalable way of stopping unauthorized vehicles at entry control points, it is also a lower-cost and easier to maintain system. Department of Defense Issues New Report on Civilian Casualty Policy

On February 1, 2019, the DOD issued a new report on civilian casualty policy. Among other things, the report directed that the Undersecretary of Defense for Policy name a senior civilian official, at or above the level of an Assistant Secretary of Defense to develop, coordinate, and oversee compliance with the policy of the Department relating to civilian casualties resulting from United States military operations.

Chinese military expert advocates using lasers to paralyze US warships in South China Sea
March 19, 2020 - The Chinese military should consider using non-lethal lasers on U.S. Navy warships to discourage their transits through the South China Sea, a state-run newspaper in China said Tuesday. Chinese military expert Song Zhongping told Global Times that the use of electromagnetic weapons, such as low-energy laser devices, could “send a strong warning” by temporarily paralyzing weapon and control systems on U.S. ships — all done “without visible conflict.”
